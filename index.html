"Biosphere"

Posted on February 23, 2024 by Collin Easley

The quote from Chapter 3 of "Sustainability Principles and Practice" by Robertson (2021), stating, "The realization that Earth is a self-regulating system has had benefits and disadvantages,"(Roberton, 2021) is something I want to respond to. To me this has me thinking about how true this statement really is. Earth can be a beautiful wonderful place but it can also be a cruel heartless place. But at the end going back to the authors point there is some equalibrum. To illustrate this look and the two photos above. One is taken by me at Torres del Paine national park in Patagonia, Chile. When I visited it was one of the most amazing places i ever been, just stunning from the deep blue water to the majestic peaks. The other photo is of the destruction by hurricane Ian in 2022. To me this is the perfect way to illustrate the contrast of earth and the eqaulium it brings. It has its stunning beauty which makes people want to preserve and protect the environment. But if there is a disturbance it causes earth to bring in natural disasters like hurricanes to shake up the established environmental order. The earth's system is in a way a call to action by humans to correct some of our wrongs and be more sustainable. In more simple words the earth's self regulation system is a parenting mechanism to make sure humans behave.

"Ecosystem and Habitat"

Posted on February 23, 2024 by Collin Easley

I will respond to the quote "When streams are not in pipes and are allowed to flow, they are active systems that inherently seek their own dynamic equilibrium" (Robinson, 2017). This gives us an insight into the behavior of streams. This quote plays to the theme of earth having an equilibrium. Streams strive for equilibrium due to the principles of ecology. Emphasizing the interconnections of systems. This means that there is a relationship of various factors from flow rates to what grows in a natural enviroment. Above is a photo of the Mississippi river and the interconnectivity of the tributaries that shows an equilibrium that it is all leading to the gulf of mexico. I find this as an interesting example of what is said in the quote that even in the big river and tributary system the quote is still relevant. This reminds of my childhood memories when I visited family in Wyoming and South Dakota where I would float off these rapids so that they always had a flow rate that would reach some equilibrium. I think of the water flowing in one direction where it would whizz over my head then it contrasted with the calm eddies. It was truly like a dance of energies trying to relate to what the quote says about water seeking equilibrium.
